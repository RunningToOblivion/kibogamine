## Problem

Find $\frac{d}{dx}(3x^4 - 2x^2 + 5)$

## Solution

Using the power rule: $\frac{d}{dx}(x^n) = nx^{n-1}$

$$\frac{d}{dx}(3x^4) = 12x^3$$

$$\frac{d}{dx}(-2x^2) = -4x$$

$$\frac{d}{dx}(5) = 0$$

Therefore: $\frac{d}{dx}(3x^4 - 2x^2 + 5) = 12x^3 - 4x$
